# Glossary

## Automotive Terms

**NHTSA**: U.S. National Highway Traffic Safety Administration. Public source for vehicle complaints, recalls, investigations, and safety data.

**Complaint**: A vehicle issue reported by a consumer, such as steering problems, braking issues, engine stalling, or electrical failures.

**Recall**: An official action when a manufacturer or regulator identifies a safety defect or standards issue that requires repair, replacement, inspection, software update, or customer notification.

**Make**: Vehicle brand, such as Toyota, Honda, Chevrolet, Tesla, or Hyundai.

**Model**: Vehicle name, such as Camry, Civic, Silverado 1500, Model 3, or Tucson.

**Model year**: The production/model version year of the vehicle.

**Component**: The vehicle system involved in the issue, such as steering, brakes, airbags, engine, electrical system, or power train.

**Consequence**: The possible outcome of a defect, such as increased crash risk.

**Remedy**: The proposed fix, such as replacing a part, updating software, or inspecting the vehicle.

## Business Analyst Terms

**Business Analyst**: A role that studies business problems, documents requirements, maps processes, works with stakeholders, and helps design better solutions.

**Stakeholder**: A person or team affected by the process, such as customer support, dealer service, product quality, compliance, or parts planning.

**Current-state process**: How the process works today.

**Future-state process**: The improved version of the process.

**Workflow**: The path work follows from one step or team to another.

**Escalation**: Moving an issue to a higher-priority team or faster response path.

**Priority score**: A score used to decide which issue should be handled first.

**SLA**: Service Level Agreement. The expected response or review time, such as 24 hours for high-priority items.

**Business requirement**: A statement of what the solution must do to solve the business problem.

**User story**: A requirement written from a user's perspective: As a [user], I want [goal], so that [benefit].

**UAT**: User Acceptance Testing. Business-user testing to confirm the solution meets requirements.

**Acceptance criteria**: Conditions that prove a user story or requirement is complete.

**KPI**: Key Performance Indicator. A metric used to monitor business performance.

**Root cause**: The underlying reason a problem happens. This project identifies recurring issue patterns, but does not prove mechanical root cause.
