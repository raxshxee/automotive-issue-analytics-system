# Process Maps

## Current-State Process

1. Customer complaint or recall signal appears.
2. Support, dealer, product, or compliance teams may review it separately.
3. Priority is judged manually.
4. Ownership may be unclear.
5. Similar issues may not be grouped quickly.
6. Service action depends on manual follow-up.

## Current-State Gaps

- No consistent priority score
- No single view of complaint and recall overlap
- No clear owner routing logic
- Manual escalation
- Limited visibility into repeated issue clusters

## Future-State Process

1. Complaint or recall data enters the system.
2. Vehicle, component, and issue theme are classified.
3. Priority score is calculated.
4. Owner team and SLA are assigned.
5. High-priority items enter a service action queue.
6. Teams review evidence and decide next action.
7. Status is monitored until closure.

## Future-State Benefits

- Faster escalation
- Less ambiguity
- Better service planning
- More consistent decision-making
- Better management visibility
