# Business Case

## Project

RecallReady Intelligence

## Simple Explanation

Car owners report vehicle problems. Regulators publish recalls. Automotive service teams need a better way to decide which recurring issues should be handled first, which team should own them, and what action should happen next.

## Business Problem

Complaint and recall signals can be scattered across customer support, dealer service, product quality, compliance, and parts planning. Without a structured review process, urgent issues can be delayed and repeated complaint patterns can be missed.

## Proposed Solution

A service operations dashboard and workflow that:

1. Collects public complaint and recall data.
2. Classifies issues by vehicle, component, and service theme.
3. Scores priority using severity, recall overlap, and volume.
4. Assigns owner teams and SLA guidance.
5. Provides a priority queue for service operations.
6. Documents requirements, user stories, and UAT cases.

## Business Value

- Faster review of high-priority issue clusters
- Clearer owner routing
- Better visibility into repeated service issues
- Stronger coordination between support, dealer service, product quality, and compliance
- More consistent decision-making

## Scope

In scope:

- Public NHTSA complaint and recall data
- Issue classification
- Priority scoring
- Service owner routing
- BA documentation
- Dashboard

Out of scope:

- Legal or mechanical safety decisions
- VIN-level outreach
- Internal manufacturer repair records
- Real-time service center integration
